Oh Dannie Boy, th' pipes, th' pipes ar callin',
Fae glen tae glen, an' doun th' ben side.
Th' summer's gaen, an' a' th' roses fallin',
Hit's ye, hit's ye maun gae an' Ah maun bide.
But come ye 'int when summer's in th' meada,
Or when th' valley's 'ushit an' white wi snaw,
Hit's a'll be 'ere in sunshine or in shada,
Oh, Dannie boy, Oh Dannie boy, Ah love ye so!!
But when ye come, an' a' th' flowers ar diein',
If Ah am deid, as deid Ah weel can be,
Yoo'll come an' fin th' stell whaur Ah am lyin',
An' kneel an' saie an Ave thonder fur me.
An' Ah sall 'ear, thoogh soft ye tread abuin me,
An' a' ma grave wull warmer, sweeter be,
Fur ye wull ben' an' tell me thon ye loo me,
An' Ah sall sleep in peace till ye come tae me!
